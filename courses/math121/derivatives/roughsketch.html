(Rough) Derivative Sketching

In this activity, draw a rough sketch of the derivative of the displayed function. There is some flexibility built-in to the answer-checking (that is, you aren't expected to be able to eyeball the exact slopes), but some criteria your sketch must meet are:

  • Correctly identify where the derivative is zero (when the original function is constant, or at local maxima/minima - where there is a horizontal tangent)
  • Have open dots where the original function is not differentiable (discontinuities and corners)
  • Where the original function is linear, have a constant derivative (positive/negative dependent on the slope of the linear segment). Further, if there are two linear segments with different slopes, the derivative should be a greater magnitude constant for the steeper linear segment
  • Have the correct sign and approach the correct infinity near vertical asymptotes
  • On a curved segment where slopes are increasing from left-to-right, the derivative should be increasing. Its sign should also correctly match the slopes of the tangents
