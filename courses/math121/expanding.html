Expanding Activity

This activity concerns expanding out products. For a refresher on the basics (the distributive property of multiplication over adddition), see Multiplication Activity, which does not involve variables.

We will extend our applications of the distibutive property to products of polynomials: sums of terms containing constants and variables, which may be raised to powers. Our strategy will be the same as for multiplying multi-digit numbers: a FOIL/rainbow, where each term in the first multiplicand is paired with each term in the second multiplicand. Observe that the exponent laws apply when multiplying two terms both of which contain a power of the same variable. In the above, multiplying \(-7y\) by \(yz^5\), we added the exponents on the \(y\)s - namely \(1+1 = 2\) - to get the exponent on \(y\) in the product, \(-7y^2z^5\).

Note that \((x + y)^2 \neq x^2 + y^2\), that is, whereas \((xy)^2 = x^2y^2\), we don't have a corresponding exponent law when an exponent is applied to a sum/difference. Instead, we must take the product of the polynomial with itself: potentially multiple times, if it is raised to an exponent higher than 2.
