Intermediate Value Theorem

In the following activity, use the Intermediate Value Theorem to show that a root or solution exists. As the problems are randomly generated, there are often quantities you couldn't evaluate without a calculator (for instance, \(2\left(\frac{\pi}}{3}\right)\) ). When inputting your solution, you need only evaluate any exact values of trigonometric functions: you can leave anything else unsimplified, or you can use a calculator to get a decimal approximation to 4 places (but first evaluate the trig functions yourself - it's good practice!)
