Limits II

We next consider limits involving infinity. The first case we are interested in is when the particular way in which the limit doesn't exist is that the values become arbitrarily large (in either the positive/negative direction), in which case we write \[\lim_{x \to n}f(x) = \pm\infty\] In the second case, we are looking at the end behaviour of a function: \[\lim_{x \to \pm\infty}f(x)\] (does the function become arbitrarily large/small? Does it approach a particular value?)
