Apple Squares

Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour
2 tsp baking powder
½ tsp salt
½ tsp cinnamon

¾ cup white sugar
¾ cup brown sugar
½ cup butter
2 eggs
2 tsp vanilla
2 cups of diced granny smith apple

2 tbsp white sugar
2 tsp cinnamon

Instructions:

Dry ingredients: in a bowl, mix together 2 cups all-purpose flour, 2 tsp baking powder, ½ tsp salt, ½ tsp cinnamon

In another bowl, mix together ¾ cup white sugar and ¾ cup brown sugar. Then mix in ½ cup butter (soften the butter by leaving it out awhile or putting in the microwave first). Use a mixer to whip them together until creamy.

Mix in 2 eggs, and 2 tsp vanilla to the bowl with the sugar-butter mixture. Then mix in the dry ingredients, about a third at a time.

Stir in 2 cups of diced granny smith apple (ideally cooking apples should be firmer apples). The moisture from the apples should gradually make the mixture a little more liquidy and easy to stir. Spread the batter into a greased 13x9 pan (grease the pan with butter).

In a separate bowl, mix together 2 tbsp white sugar and 2 tsp cinnamon. Sprinkle this mixture on top of the batter in the pan evenly.

Bake at 350F for 30-35 minutes approximately. You can test whether it's done or not by lightly touching the cake with your fingertips. If you find that it springs back up a little bit when you press down, then it's either done or nearly done. (Or you can just cut into the center and try some of the cake!)

Let cool on a cooling rack, cut into squares and enjoy!
