Double Chocolate Cookies

Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour
¾ cups cocoa
1 tsp baking soda
½ tsp salt

2/3 cup sugar
2/3 cup brown sugar
1 cup butter
2 eggs
1 tbsp vanilla
2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

Dry ingredients: in a bowl, mix together 2 cups all-purpose flour, ¾ cups cocoa, 1 tsp baking soda, and ½ tsp salt

In a separate bowl, mix together 2/3 cup sugar and 2/3 cup brown sugar, then 1 cup butter until creamy.

Beat in 2 eggs, and 1 tbsp vanilla. Then beat in the dry ingredients.

Stir in the 2 cups of semi-sweet chocolate chips.

Store covered in the fridge when not in use. Form rounded mounds of cookie dough on a cookie sheet of approximately equal size, about a tablespoon worth. The cookies will spread while baking, so leave room between them. Bake about 9-11 minutes (depending on size of the cookies) at 350F (you can tell they are definitely done if they start cracking).

I test cookies for doneness by running a butter knife along the top of them: if they have developed a "crust" and don't depress too much, they will finish cooking in the 5 minutes you leave them on the pan after removing from the oven. Then, move them to a cooling rack.
